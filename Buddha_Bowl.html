Buddha Bowl

Home

Description

A Buddha Bowl is a colorful, nourishing meal served in a single bowl, layered with whole grains, vibrant vegetables, and plant-based proteins. It’s designed to be visually balanced, filling, and naturally wholesome, offering a mix of textures and fresh flavors in every bite.

Perfect for lunch or dinner, this modern dish celebrates variety and simplicity at the same time. Packed with vitamins, fiber, and slow-release energy, a Buddha Bowl is an easy way to eat healthy without sacrificing taste or creativity.

Ingredients

  • 1 cup cooked quinoa (or brown rice)
  • 1 cup roasted sweet potato cubes
  • 1 cup steamed broccoli florets
  • ½ cup shredded purple cabbage
  • ½ cup cherry tomatoes, halved
  • ½ cup cooked chickpeas (rinsed & drained if canned)
  • ¼ avocado, sliced
  • 2 tablespoons tahini
  • Juice of ½ lemon
  • 1 teaspoon olive oil
  • Salt & pepper to taste
  • Optional toppings: sesame seeds, fresh herbs (parsley, cilantro)

Steps

  1. Cook the quinoa (or brown rice) according to package instructions and set aside to cool slightly.
  2. Preheat the oven to 200°C (400°F), toss sweet potato cubes with a drizzle of olive oil, salt, and pepper, and roast for about 20–25 minutes until tender.
  3. Steam the broccoli florets for 4–5 minutes until bright green and slightly crisp.
  4. Prepare the chickpeas by rinsing and draining; optionally sauté them with a pinch of paprika for extra flavor.
  5. Slice the avocado and halve the cherry tomatoes; shred the cabbage if not already prepared.
  6. Whisk together tahini, lemon juice, a splash of water, and a pinch of salt to make the dressing.
  7. Assemble the bowl: place quinoa as the base, then neatly arrange sweet potato, broccoli, chickpeas, cabbage, tomatoes, and avocado on top.
  8. Drizzle with the tahini dressing and sprinkle sesame seeds or fresh herbs if desired.
  9. Serve immediately and enjoy your colorful, nourishing Buddha Bowl.
